PDF Printing Mistakes to Avoid — and How to Fix Them

Published: April 8, 2026  ·  5 min read  ·  GPTPayer Team

Printing a PDF seems straightforward — but it is surprisingly easy to end up with cut-off text, blurry images, or pages printing in the wrong order. This guide covers the most common PDF printing problems and the exact steps to fix each one.

Problem 1: Content Is Getting Cut Off at the Edges

This happens when the PDF's page size does not match the paper size in the printer, or when the margins are too small. Here is how to fix it:

  • In the print dialog, select "Fit to page" or "Scale to fit" instead of "Actual size."
  • Alternatively, choose "Shrink to printable area" to let the printer automatically adjust the scale.
  • The better long-term fix: when creating the PDF, set appropriate margins — at least 10mm on all sides.
💡 Always Preview First: Before sending a document to the printer, check "Print Preview." If text or images appear near the edges, adjust the settings before wasting paper.

Problem 2: Images Are Printing Blurry

For sharp printed images, you need a minimum of 150 DPI — and ideally 300 DPI for professional results. If the original image was low resolution, the print will be blurry. The printer cannot create detail that does not exist in the source file. Always use high-resolution images when preparing documents for print.

Duplex (Double-Sided) Printing

Duplex printing saves paper and looks more professional for multi-page documents. In the print dialog, look for "Print on both sides" or "Two-sided printing." There are two binding options:

  • Long-edge binding: For standard portrait documents — pages flip like a book.
  • Short-edge binding: For landscape documents — pages flip from the top, like a notepad.

Printing Multiple Pages Per Sheet

For handouts, drafts, or reference sheets, you can print 2, 4, 6, or even 9 pages on a single sheet of paper. Find this option under "Pages per sheet" in the print dialog. The text will be smaller, but it is a great way to save paper when print quality is not the top priority.

Color vs. Black and White

Color printing is significantly more expensive than black and white — both in ink and per-page cost. If your document is primarily text, always print in black and white. The output will look essentially identical for text-only documents. Reserve color printing for documents that genuinely require it, such as those containing photographs or color-coded charts.

Always Print the PDF Directly — Not a Screenshot

Some people take a screenshot of a PDF and print the screenshot. This is a mistake — the screenshot will have lower resolution than the original, resulting in noticeably softer text and images. Always open the original PDF file and print directly from it.
