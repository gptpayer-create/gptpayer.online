Frequently Asked Questions

Why choose PNG over JPG for PDF conversion?
PNG is lossless — it preserves every pixel perfectly. JPG uses lossy compression, which can blur text and fine details. For PDFs with text, diagrams, or charts, PNG is the better choice.
What does the transparent background option do?
When enabled, the PDF page background becomes transparent instead of white. This is useful for layering PDF content over other images in design tools like Figma, Photoshop, or Canva.
Will 400 DPI create very large files?
Yes. At 400 DPI each page can be several megabytes. For web use, 72-150 DPI is more efficient. Reserve 300-400 DPI for print-quality archiving.
Is my PDF uploaded to any server?
No. Everything is processed in your browser using PDF.js. Your file never leaves your device, making it completely private and secure.
