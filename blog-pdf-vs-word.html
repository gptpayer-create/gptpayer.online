PDF vs Word (DOCX): When to Use Which Format?

Published: April 12, 2026  ·  5 min read  ·  GPTPayer Team

Thousands of people search for this question every day — "Should I send my resume as a PDF or Word file?" or "Does my college want documents in PDF or DOCX format?" It sounds like a simple question, but the wrong answer can cause your document to display incorrectly — or even get rejected. Let's settle this once and for all.

When to Use PDF

Use PDF whenever you are sharing a finalized document and you do not want anyone to modify it. PDF looks identical on every device, every operating system, and every screen size. The main use cases for PDF include:

  • Resumes and CVs: Always send as PDF. A Word file can look completely different on another person's computer if they have a different version of Office or different fonts installed.
  • Official applications: Government forms, college applications, visa documents, bank submissions.
  • Invoices and receipts: Send clients a PDF so they cannot edit the amounts or terms.
  • Presentations shared externally: If the recipient may not have PowerPoint, a PDF is a safe fallback.
  • Certificates, contracts, and legal documents.
💡 The Golden Rule: If the document is final and editing is not needed, use PDF. If editing is expected or required, use Word.

When to Use Word (DOCX)

Word format is the right choice when the recipient needs to edit the document or when you are working collaboratively:

  • Collaborating on a document with your team
  • Sending content to an editor for review or feedback
  • Sharing a template that others will fill in
  • Using Track Changes or Comments for revision rounds
  • When a client or institution explicitly requests DOCX format

Resumes: PDF or Word?

This is the most common debate. The short answer: use PDF in 90% of cases. Only send a Word file if the job posting specifically states "DOCX format preferred" or "please submit in Word format." With PDF, your resume will look exactly as you designed it — fonts, spacing, layout — regardless of what software the recruiter uses to open it.

File Size Differences

Word files are generally smaller than PDFs for text-heavy documents. However, if a PDF contains optimized images, it can actually be smaller than its Word equivalent. For email attachments, both formats are widely accepted, so file size is rarely a concern in practice.

Security: PDF Has the Edge

PDF files can be password protected and are not easily editable without specialized software. Word documents, on the other hand, can be opened and modified by almost anyone with a computer. For contracts, agreements, and any document where authenticity matters, always share as PDF.
