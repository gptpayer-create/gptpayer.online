How to Scan Documents with Your Phone — The Right Way (2026)

Published: April 10, 2026  ·  5 min read  ·  GPTPayer Team

Almost everyone has a smartphone, but very few people have a dedicated document scanner. The good news is that modern smartphone cameras are powerful enough to produce near-professional scan quality — as long as you know the right techniques. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Best Apps for Document Scanning

Android:

  • Google Drive: Has a built-in scanner — completely free, automatic perspective correction, direct PDF export. Tap the "+" button and select "Scan."
  • Microsoft Lens: Excellent document detection, exports to Word, PDF, and OneNote. Free.
  • Adobe Scan: AI-powered with OCR (text recognition), free with an Adobe account.

iPhone:

  • Notes App: Built-in scanner — open a note, tap the camera icon, and select "Scan Documents."
  • Files App: Can scan directly to PDF.
  • Microsoft Lens: Also available on iOS with the same great quality.
💡 Recommendation: Android users should try Google Drive's scanner first — it is free, fast, and produces consistently good results. iPhone users will find the built-in Notes scanner more than sufficient for everyday documents.

7 Tips for a Perfect Scan Every Time

  1. Get the lighting right: Use bright, even lighting. Avoid direct sunlight — it creates harsh shadows. Scanning under a ceiling light indoors usually gives the best results.
  2. Lay the document flat: Flatten any creases or folds before scanning. Place the document on a hard, flat surface.
  3. Hold the camera perfectly straight: Position your phone directly above the document, not at an angle. Scanning apps will auto-correct perspective, but starting straight produces better results.
  4. Use a contrasting background: Place white documents on a dark surface — a dark table or dark notebook works well. This helps the app detect the document edges accurately.
  5. Check focus before capturing: Make sure the text is sharp and clearly in focus before tapping the capture button.
  6. Scan all pages in one session: Do not close the app between pages. Most scanning apps let you add multiple pages to a single session, creating a multi-page PDF automatically.
  7. Review before sending: Always zoom into the scanned image to confirm the text is legible before sharing or submitting.

What Is OCR and Why Does It Matter?

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It is a technology that converts scanned images into searchable, selectable text. A standard scan is just a picture — you cannot search within it or copy text from it. With OCR, the scanned document becomes a proper text document. You can use Ctrl+F to search for words, copy and paste text, and the file becomes accessible to screen readers for visually impaired users.
